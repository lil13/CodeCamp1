Steve Jobs

Photo by Steve Jobs

Stephen Paul "Steve" Jobs is an American entrepreneur who has been widely recognized as a pioneer of the IT era. One of the founders, chairman of the board and CEO of Apple. One of the founders and CEO of Pixar. In the late 1970s, Steve and his friend Steve Wozniak developed one of the first personal computers to have great commercial potential. The Apple II computer became the first mass-produced product of Apple, created on the initiative of Steve Jobs. Jobs later saw the commercial potential of a mouse-controlled graphical interface, which led to the advent of the Apple Lisa and, a year later, the Macintosh (Mac). After losing the power struggle to the board in 1985, Jobs left Apple and founded NeXT, a company that develops a computer platform for universities and businesses. In 1986, he acquired the computer graphics division of Lucasfilm, turning it into a Pixar studio. He remained Pixar's CEO and major shareholder until the studio was acquired by the Walt Disney Company in 2006, making Jobs the largest private shareholder and board member of Disney.
